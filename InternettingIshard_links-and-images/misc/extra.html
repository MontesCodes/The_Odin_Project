Character Sets

You can use UTF-8 to support a wide range of characters from different languages and count in Turkish:

  1. bir
  2. iki
  3. üç
  4. dört
  5. beş
  6. altı
  7. yedi
  8. sekiz
  9. dokuz
  10. on

HTML Entities

You can use HTML entities to display special characters, like © for the copyright symbol, ♥ for a heart, and € for the euro sign. Also < for less than, > for greater than, & for ampersand, " for double quote, and ' for single quote, and > for greater than, and “ for left double quotation mark, and ” for right double quotation mark, and ‘ for left single quotation mark, and ’ for right single quotation mark.


If you’re into “web typography,” you’ll also find yourself using curly quotes quite a bit.
